Introduction

A Hidden Paradise
in East Borneo

The Derawan Archipelago is a cluster of islands nestled in Berau Regency, East Kalimantan. Renowned for its breathtaking underwater world, these islands are home to thousands of marine species — from green sea turtles and stingless jellyfish to giant manta rays. Each island weaves together natural splendour, indigenous wisdom, and lasting conservation.

Why Derawan

Six Reasons to Visit

01
Marine Biodiversity

Over 870 fish species and 460 coral species make Derawan one of the world's greatest marine biodiversity hotspots.

02
Sea Turtle Nesting

Witness green and hawksbill sea turtles come ashore nearly every night — a rare and unforgettable encounter.

03
Jellyfish Lake

Kakaban hosts one of only two stingless jellyfish lakes on Earth — a living testament to isolated evolution.

04
Diving & Snorkelling

Visibility up to 30 metres and calm currents create perfect conditions for beginners and expert divers alike.

05
Bajo Sea Culture

Connect with the Bajo people — nomadic sea dwellers whose traditions and maritime wisdom span generations.

06
Ecotourism

Every visit directly supports turtle conservation, coral restoration, and sustainable livelihoods for local communities.

Natural Wonders
The Archipelago

Four Crown Jewels

Each island carries its own character and wonder, forming an irreplaceable harmony

01 / 04 — DERAWAN

Pulau Derawan

The gateway island of the archipelago. Derawan's white-sand shores let you swim alongside friendly green sea turtles by day. At night, dozens of turtles emerge from the sea to nest on the very same beach.

Green TurtlesSnorkellingOverwater ResortSunset Pier
02 / 04 — SANGALAKI

Pulau Sangalaki

A world-renowned manta ray sanctuary. Sangalaki's "Manta Point" sees aggregations of oceanic giants year-round. A weathered colonial lighthouse crowns the interior — an iconic photography destination.

Manta RaysScuba DivingHawksbill TurtleHistoric Lighthouse
03 / 04 — KAKABAN

Pulau Kakaban

Home to the legendary Jellyfish Lake. A 5 km² inland saltwater lake holds millions of stingless jellyfish — an isolated population that lost their venom through millennia of evolution. Only two such lakes exist on Earth.

Jellyfish LakeJungle TrekRare EcosystemConservation Zone
04 / 04 — MARATUA

Pulau Maratua

The largest atoll in the archipelago, cradling a mesmerising turquoise lagoon. Maratua offers world-class wall diving, pristine hidden beaches, and exclusive over-water resorts — also a base for international marine biology research.

Luxury ResortsWall DivingBlue LagoonResearch Station

Conservation Efforts

A commitment to protecting the natural life and marine ecosystems of Derawan for future generations

Our Programme

Integrated Conservation & Community

Derawan's conservation programme works in tandem with local communities and is supported by international organisations. Every visitor directly contributes to preserving these irreplaceable marine ecosystems.

  • Sea Turtle Protection

    Daily monitoring of nesting turtles and protected incubation of eggs until hatching.

  • Coral Reef Restoration

    Coral transplantation and artificial reef programmes rebuild damaged reef structures.

  • Zero-Waste Tourism

    Plastic reduction campaigns and responsible waste management across all islands.

  • Community Empowerment

    Training and mentoring local communities in sustainable ecotourism practices.

Under the Surface

A World Within
a World

Below Derawan's glassy surface lies a labyrinth of colour and life. Ancient coral cathedrals rise from the seabed, home to creatures found nowhere else on Earth.

Currents here are gentle, temperatures warm, and the sheer density of marine life is humbling. World-class dive instructors operate across all four islands.

Travel Guide

Best Time to Visit

March — October

Dry season with calm seas, clear skies, and peak underwater visibility.

Year-Round

Sea turtles nest on Derawan's beaches almost every night in any season.

February — April

Peak manta ray season at Sangalaki, with aggregations of dozens of gentle giants.

Getting There

How to Reach Derawan

1

Fly into Berau

Flights from Jakarta, Surabaya, or Balikpapan arrive at Kalimarau Airport. Journey: 2–3 hours depending on origin.

2

Road Transfer to Tanjung Batu

A scenic 2.5-hour overland drive takes you from the airport to Tanjung Batu harbour along the East Borneo coast.

3

Speedboat to the Islands

A 45–60 minute speedboat crossing over vivid blue water delivers you directly to Pulau Derawan.
